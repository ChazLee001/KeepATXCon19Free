FAQ COVID-19

  • What is COVID-19?

    An infectious respiratory disease. Reference: CDC

  • Where does the name COVID-19 come from?

    COVID-19 was named by the World Health Organization (WHO) based on their established naming conventions (the 19 stands for the year it was first discovered). Reference: WHO

  • How does COVID-19 get spread?

    Mainly person-to-person: Between people who are in close contact with one another (within about 6 feet - twice the length of an iPhone charging cord). Through droplets produced when an infected person coughs or sneezes. These droplets can land in the mouths or noses of people who are nearby and possibly be inhaled into the lungs. Reference: CDC

  • Is there a vaccine for COVID-19?

    There is currently no vaccine or drug available to prevent or cure COVID-19. The best way to prevent illness is to avoid being exposed to this virus (like most other viruses), but you can take certain actions that can help prevent the spread of COVID-19. Reference: CDC

  • What should I do if I come in contact with someone with COVID-19?

    If you think you may have been exposed to COVID-19, you should contact a healthcare provider immediately. To find a healthcare provider, see our Resources page. It is important to call before going into a clinic, urgent care, or emergency department. Giving these places a heads up before you arrive will help to prevent any potential spread of COVID-19.
